ABSTRACT

Until now, typically gene regulation have been identified which include:- (I) Rate of Gene transcription and mRNA decay determining the steady state level of transcript. (II) Transcription along with translation rate determine the amount of protein produced. (III) Transcription is controlled by DNA Binding Protein (DBP), post-transcriptional regulation such as m-RNA decay and translation is mediated by RNA Binding Protein (RBP) which form ribonucleoprotein complex with transcripts. Gene expression steps are highly co-ordinated and interdependent. Different techniques which are present to find global RNA-protein interaction network are not able to uncover this task because of many reason which are given below: (a) Available interaction data cover a quite small fraction of known RBPs. (b) Experimentally determined interaction are often noisy and cell line dependent. Even for RBP with experimentally determined interactomes, information are not fully accurate. Due to dependency of those techniques on expression level and cell lines, some kind of interaction might be missed. (c) These techniques individuate the binding sites of single RBP of interest in each experiment. Exception is gPar-CLIP. That determine mRNA-bound proteome and its global occupancy profile. gPar-CLIP does not allow match binding sites with specific RBPs, thus it does not provide information how multiple RBP target same mRNAs. Similarly, PTR combiner extract cluster of mRNA co-regulator from interaction data. It employ a pattern set mining techniques based on boolean matrix factorization to extract cluster of co-regulator RBPs. It also provide biological analysis of extracted cluster that might suggest functional characterization of set of mRNAs targeted by cluster of regulators and of the binding dynamics of different RBPs belong to the some cluster. Map CLIP binding site of 60 RBP on human genome to construct RBP-RNA network. Examine correlation of each RBPs expression at both transcripts and protein level with target RNA to see how these correlation patterns changes.Using modeling techniques like multivariate regression modeling stepwise linear regression and elastic net to analyzed deflect factor impacting change in expression pattern through two level Analysis. RBP at both proteomic and transcriptomic level play an important role in coordinating expression change of target RNA. This is defined by various factors govering functions of these RBPs. To understand how RBPs are associated with target, we correlate expression of one of protein coding transcript of each RBP with expression of target transcripts across 18 human tissues and compared it with control set of transcripts.
INTRODUCTION

MicroRNAs (miRNAs) are ~21 to ~24 nucleotide RNA molecules, play important regulators of post transcriptional gene expression and have been involved in different cellular pathways (Bushati and Cohen, 2007). The biogenesis of miRNAs is a complex process primarily dependent by RNA binding proteins (RBPs). The canonical miRNA biogenesis start with Precursor miRNAs (Pre-miRNAs) hairpins, which are embedded within exonic and intronic regions of host protein-coding and non-coding primary miRNAs (Pri-miRNAs) transcripts, are processed by microprocessor complex. microprocessor complex comprehend with RNase III DROSHA and the double-stranded (ds) RBP DGCR8 (Han et al., 2004; Gregory et al., 2004; Denli et al., 2004). DROSHA cleaves off flanking single stranded sequences and generates a miRNA precursor that is transported to the cytoplasm from nucleus by exportin-5 protein, which is also a RBP mostly helps in transportation (Kim et al., 2009). In the cytoplasm, the RNase III DICER1 binds loop region of pre-miRNAs and cleaved it to produce the mature 3p/5p miRNA duplex . DICER1 is a RNase III enzyme which contains an ATPase/RNA helicase, a PAZ domain (Yan et al.,2003), two catalytic RNase III domains, and a C-terminal dsRBD (Lau et al.,2012). With the help of the dsRBPs TRBP or PACT, either 3p or 5p strand is selected and loaded into the RNA induced silencing complex(RISC), where it directly interacts with a member of the Argonaute protein family (Dueck and Meister, 2014; Kim et al.,2009). Argonautes (AGO 1-4) are bi-lobed proteins, they consist of an N-terminal , a PAZ domain (similar to the PAZ domain found in Dicer), a middle (MID) domain and a PIWI domain (Song et al.,2004; Elkayam et al., 2012; Schirle et al. , 2012). Ago2 (one of the four mammalian Argonautes) is the only Ago that possesses endonucleolytic activity and can initiate the cleavage of the passenger strand of an extensively base-paired small RNA duplex or the targeted mRNA if it is perfectly complementary to the Ago2 bound small RNA (Song et al.,2004; Matranga et al.,2005; Yuen et al.,2005; Parker et al.,2004; Wang et al., 2008).

An increasing amount of evidence also suggests that cleavage competent Argonautes also play a role in the processing of a pri-miRNA in C. elegans and in the Dicer dependent and independent maturation of pre-miRNAs in mammals and worms (Zisoulis et al.,2012; Diederichs et al.,2007; Bouasker et al.,2012; Cifuentes et al.2010).RBPs could also regulate miRNA biogenesis by affecting the expression or stability of the canonical proteins of the miRNA pathway. Cells have the ability to utilize a wide spectrum of mechanisms to produce functional miRNAs. Although the afore mentioned canonical pathway accounts for the production of most miRNAs, it has also been shown that there exist alternative pathways for miRNA biogenesis, which bypass a part of the biogenesis steps mentioned above.

These miRNAs are therefore termed non-canonical miRNAs. The processing and action of miRNAs are extensively regulated by auxiliary factors to ensure cell/tissue specific functions or adequate response to environmental and cellular stimuli. One of the largest groups of proteins that influence the miRNA pathways are RNA-binding proteins (RBPs). Similar to protein-coding genes, non-coding pri-miRNAs expression is subject to post-transcriptional control. Yet, only a small fraction (1%–5%) of RBPs have been demonstrated to control the various stages of miRNA processing in an endogenous context, though till now there are 5000 RBPs have been identified in human (Ghosh et al.,2018) . Jha et al, (2011), while working to understand the process of post-transcriptional regulation, processing and handling of miRNA forms, using expression data and scanning for some known RBP motifs they found that miRNAs have potential very high relationship with RBPs, and several RBPs could be involved in miRNA formation and translocation besides well known Drosha/Dicer/Argonaute proteins. While the RBPs responsible for the general processing of miRNAs namely, DROSHA and DGCR8 are very well characterized, where other RBPs can also play a more important role in miRNA biogenesis to affect a specific subset of miRNAs. Now, very recently due to technological advancement some NGS methods have been developed where transcriptome wide RBP interactome could be discovered by using a variety of cross-linking immuno-precipitations (CLIP-seq). These methods include PAR-CLIP/HITS-CLIP/iCLIP/eCLIP/ir-CLIP CLEAR-CLIP/miCLIP/PAR-iCLIP/PAR-CLIP-MeRIP etc. However, despite of their high-throughput nature, these methods are specifically designed for any given RBP and don't provide information about all RBPs. Although a number of RNA-binding proteins have been implicated in miRNA biogenesis, a comprehensive picture of the dynamic interactome of pri-/pre-miRNAs is still missing. Therefore, using the above mentioned experiments one can get enough data to model the interactions of any given number of RBPs. To gain a full understanding of miRNAs processing there is a need to identify and classify additional regulators of miRNA biogenesis. Thus far, systematic and comprehensive approaches to identify RBP:miRNA interactions have been rare and limited in their utility. The present study has realized these developments and proposes a solution to decipher miRNAs and their RBP association in miRNA biogenesis . In this paper we have presented the regulatory models for RBP dependent miRNA biogenesis models and characterized miRNAs based on their RBP association considering multi-experimental conditions. which include different experimental conditions (disease/control) and different tissue types of human.

MATERIAL and METHOD

Data for miRNA-seq and RNA-seq based high throughput studies were collected from Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) and Sequence Read Archive (SRA) for 20 experiments which included 47 different experimental conditions. Complete list of various experimental conditions (for both RNA-seq and miRNA-seq) and source of data along with detail experimental description are available in Supplementary Table-1.

The miRNA sequences of human (1,881 precursor miRNA; 2,588 mature miRNA) are collected from the the mirBase database version 21 (Kozomara et al., 2010). 1230 CLIP-seq experiments high confidence processed data for 155 RBPs are collected from ENCODE and GEO database. These datasets based on different types of CLIP-seq experiments such as: PAR-CLIP, PAR-CLIP-MeRIP, HITS-CLIP, eCLIP, iCLIP, CLEAR-CLIP and irCLIP techniques which include different experimental conditions, the detail description of datasets are presented in Table-2.

RNA-seq and miRNA seq data were filtered using filteR (Gahlan et al.,2012) and trimometric (Bolgar et al., 2014). For mapping RNA-seq reads across the human genome (hg38) Seqmap has been used which is based on Bowtie platform. The alignment result was saved in BAM format for quantification. RSEQ tool (Jiang et al.,2009) has been implemented for quantification of gene expression from BAM files. For expression analysis of Pre-miRNAs same RNA-seq pipeline was carry out, but here RNA-seq reads were mapped over pre-miRNA sequences collected from mirBase and the alignment result are store in a BAM file. RSEQ tool was used for quantification of Pre-miRNA expression from RNA-seq data . MirDeep2 tool (Marc et al.,2012) was used for expression analysis of mature miRNA from miRNA-seq data. For mapping miRNA-seq reads over mature miRNAs collected from mirBase mapper.pl script has been used. Quantifier.pl script has been executed for quantification of mature miRNA expression from the saved BAM files. The details pipeline followed for expression analysis in Pre-miRNA, RBPs and mature miRNA are presented in Fig 2. Out of 155 RBPs 46 RBPs are collected from ENCODE database which include 222 experiments . All bed files of 46 RBPs are collected from ENCODE , which does not require further processing. Besides these 46 RBPs, CLIP-seq data for 109 RBPs are collected from GEO which included 1008 experiments . All these raw reads are processed following a standard protocol and the detail pipeline presented in fig.2.

To get the regulon of each RBP, initially possible partners of those 155 RBPs have been collected from String database (Szklarczyk, D. et al.) which include RBPs and genes. There are total 1169 (804 RBPs + 365 genes) possible partners of these 155 RBPs are collected and we don't have the CLIP-seq data for other RBPs besides these 155 RBPs. so there is a need to establish a general relationship between the binding sites and expression (up/down) of these RBPs. To establish a general view CLIP-seq data and RNA-seq data in same experimental conditions collected for 73 RBPs and the detail description of these data are presented in Table-3. Similarly RNA-seq expression analysis has been performed to quantify the expression of RBP in that particular condition. Correlation analysis has been carry out to check the association between binding sites and expression for different RBPs in different experimental conditions.

Due to few number of samples in our study, we had decided a correlation 0.8 for strong association. Those RBPs showed distinctive behavior in correlation i.e. in some conditions correlation greater than 0.8 where as in other conditions it is less than 0.8 . The difference in correlation of same RBP in different conditions might be due to interference of other gene or RBPs ,which impact the expression and binding of that particular RBP. To address this distinctive behavior of correlation in different conditions of same RBP and to figure out possible interaction (synergistic/antagonistic) with other RBPs or genes a co-expression network analysis has been carried out using a in-house prepared R-script. To perform the co-expression analysis, initially the possible partners were collected for each RBPs from the string database. Again based on the expression data of RBP and their partners in particular condition, their interaction (both positive/negative ) has been evaluated from co-expression network. To study the involvement of possible RBPs and their regulons in miRNA biogenesis a Bayesian Network Anlysis (BNA) has been performed based on expression data of miRNAs, RBPs and possible partners of RBPs collected from string database. BNA has been performed for all 47 experimental conditions and the input consisting of 1881 Pre-miRNAs, 2588 mature miRNAs , 155 RBPs and 1169 genes . But in each experimental condition , those Pre-miRNA and mature miRNA are expressed were considered. We selected those RBPs which have binding site on those miRNAs expressed in that particular experimental condition, also we included those RBPs and genes regulating RBPs having binding sites on those miRNAs. Bayesian network is a type of probabilistic graphical model that represents a set of variables and their conditional dependencies via a directed acyclic graph (DAG). The basic model is a p-dimensional random vector X with joint distribution P. Refer to the main idea of miRNA biogenesis, a more general approach is adopted via structural equation modelling (SEM) . in this approach , each conditional probability distribution(CPD) is directly modeled via a generalized linear model. The basic steps followed are structure learning for possible DAGs using the expression data, sparse regularized SEM based on the CPD and decision on a suitable penalty to model high dimensional data (n is very small as compare to p) are presented in fig. 4.

RESULT AND DISCUSSION

In human cells, most miRNAs are transcribed by RNAP II as Pri-miRNAs (Bartel,2004), which range from a few hundred nucleotides to several kilobases (kb). Some miRNAs are transcribed by their own promoter but the majority are found within introns (Bartel & Chen,2004). In our study 1kb flanking region around Pre-miRNA had considered, which we assume as putative Pri-miRNA due to limited resources regarding availability of Pri-miRNAs . To find out the binding site of RBPs on Pri-miRNA bed files of 46 RBPs which included 222 CLIP-seq samples were intersects with Pri-miRNAs and Pre-miRNAs using the Bed-tools intersect and the data collected from GEO for rest of 109 RBPs which included 1008 CLIP-seq samples were mapped on both Pri-miRNAs and Pre-miRNAs using Bowtie2. The detail statistics of binding sites of each RBP on different Pri-miRNA and Pre-miRNA are provided in the supplementary Table-4 and detail of number of RBPs bind in each Pri-miRNA and Pre-miRNA are provided in Table-5. Though we know that DROSHA is involved for processing of Pre-miRNA form Pri-miRNA , but it has been noticed that less number of Pri-miRNAs have binding sites of DROSHA . To check the status of DROSHA in Pre-miRNA processing from Pri-miRNA further a correlation analysis has been performed based on the expression data of Pre-miRNA and DROSHA in our data which include 47 experimental conditions. From the correlation analysis we found 1376 Pre-miRNAs has significant positive correlation with DROSHA, which indicates that DROSHA have been involved in Pre-miRNA processing from Pri-miRNA. From both approaches it has been noticed that there are 361 Pri-miRNAs are common, after discarding 361 Pri-miRNA from 495 Pri-miRNA we left with 134 Pri-miRNA. About website

RBP-miRNA Crosstalking web-portal is a first of the dynamic portal which provide information about gene-regulation information achieved by interaction or crosstalking between RBP, precursor and mature miRNA. This portal take tissue or condition specific RBP and miRNA. This generate network from it.

Technology Used

The portal is implemented using advance libraries of HTML5. Portal homepage and background pages are developed in HTML5/CSS3, JQuery/JavaScript with support of Bootstrap packages. The website provides the facility for gaining insight . All the interaction can be visualized in selective mode. Common target analysis is also activated for miRNAs which can be extended to the network. All the interaction can be visualized in selective mode. All the network elements can be selected for pathways and functional enrichment analysis. The analysis can be done in comparative mode. The network relationships between nodes and edges are structured into JSON file via neomodel module of python comprising django webframework. A set of full network JSON file is loaded into D3, a web based visualization open library. Query sent by a user from HTML pages is processed through PHP in the background and retrieves results of respective sections

Network generated & Customize

Dynamic network generated at real time and customize the network by query based specific information generated by the user using Cypher query implimented in neomodal specific PYTHON driver for NEO4J Database.
